Reflection Blog of first two topics

March 13, 2026

We began with the First Topic named “What is Information?” and learned various things within this topic. We have begun with DIKW Hierarchy, a model which describes how facts can be turned into understanding and improved decision-making. The DIKW model is an approach to explain how raw data are converted into valuable information and knowledge and wisdom that help us make better decisions. Raw facts constitute data. Data include figures, measurements or observations. Without any interpretation, raw facts are just plain numbers. For example temperature readings taken throughout the day are pure data. With some processing raw facts become information and through that we understand what the data signify. The next step is knowledge which can be acquired through interpretation of information by means of experience, learning and understanding. Wisdom is another stage in the chain after the knowledge because wisdom utilizes knowledge to make wise decisions. Basically the DIKW model outlines the process of how we use data to extract information, create knowledge, and then make decisions based on the gained knowledge. Another key point that we learned is creating blogs for Reflection. In the end of the unit we started a new unit entitled “Nature of Information Problems” and we have learned several topics including Brightness and contrast, Geometric transformations in images, Optimization and formal analysis, Modeling and abstraction etc. The first topic covered during this unit was the representation of images and pixels. A digital image is usually defined in a computer as a two dimensional grid of pixels. Each pixel carries the color information and thepixels play an essential role in modern computer technologies. The digital images in computers can be defined using color values. For example images are often stored in computers using RGB and each color value usually ranges from 0 to 255.The following topic covered the aspects of brightness, contrast and image histograms. They help in enhancing the visibility of images. The process of brightness involves changing the lightness of an image whereas the latter focuses on increasing the difference between the light and dark areas of an image. In the final session I gained knowledge on some geometric transformations that include rotations, scaling, and reflections. The above transformations chnage the size and orientation of images while maintaining their structure. The two topics have enabled me to gain a better understanding of the basics of digital imaging creation, manipulation etc. We also did some optimizations and solved some optimization problems. Overall these two topics were new for me but I found the second one slightly more difficult due to the concepts of images and pixels. This subject required a considerable amount of reading and analysis which eventually gave me some understanding on the topic. However I believe that all other sub topics could be understood better due to some knowledge of some topics from school earlier. For example solving optimization and geometric transformation problems involved basic mathematical calculations which I had already been taught before.
